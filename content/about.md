            <p>I like to think of myself as a <strong>curious soul</strong>. I know what I love, but I'm always open to talking about <em>literally anything</em>. People have told me they sometimes felt like they were being interviewed by me, and that my questions could feel a little <em>intense</em>. I promise I'm just <strong>genuinely interested</strong> in your passions. If you spend your weekends documenting abandoned buildings, I will genuinely want to know <em>everything</em> about it.</p>
            <p>I'm originally from a small city in the greater <strong>Bari</strong> area, in the beautiful region of <strong>Apulia, Italy</strong>, mostly famous for its <strong>Burrata</strong>, which has since gone on to conquer the world.</p>
            <p>Moving to Paris was the perfect excuse to finally <em>play Fortnite all night without my mom yelling at me</em>. More seriously, it was to pursue my studies at <strong>Université Paris 1 Panthéon-Sorbonne</strong>, where I studied <strong>philosophy</strong>. A lot of thinking was involved, <em>sometimes too much</em>, on things that maybe didn't need it.</p>
            <p>My first <strong>Erasmus experience</strong> changed something in me. I'd always been drawn to foreign languages and cultures (I even attended an international language school growing up), but living abroad and collaborating with different <strong>NGOs</strong> sparked a deeper, more political curiosity about what it means to share a <strong>collective European identity</strong>.</p>
            <p>I attended many <strong>Erasmus+</strong> projects as a participant and enjoyed exploring different topics going from theatre to business courses. But as always I wanted to know more. I wanted to understand how it works being <em>on the other side</em>.</p>
            <p>I was lucky enough to be able to collaborate on the implementation of a mobility and get a glimpse of what it means to set everything up. Turns out I enjoy <strong>making things run smoothly</strong> almost as much as I enjoy <em>overthinking them</em>.</p>
            <p>For now, this website is my little <strong>side project</strong>. I'm building it, writing on it, and figuring out the rest as I go. If you want to see what's been on my mind lately, the <strong>blog</strong> is a good place to start.</p>
